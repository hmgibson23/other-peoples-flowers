Other People's Flowers

A podcast for stories, essays, and poems.

About

Other People's Flowers is a weekly podcast to give voice to stories, essays, or poems.

Some people like to listen to stories, some people prefer to read them. We're for the people who like to listen to stories.

We give writier's voice by reading their work out on our podcast. We don't mind where you're from, who you are, what you do, and what your political inclincations might be. We just want writing you want to be read out loud.

If you'd like to have your piece showcased, please see our submission guidelines below.

Submit

Please send your rejected pieces to editor@otherpeoplesflowers.org. Please include the title of the work and your name in the email title.

Pieces should be no longer than 6,000 words.

Manuscripts should be double-spaced, 12pt font, preferably Times New Roman. Formats can be .doc, .docx, .pdf, or .odt. We actually prefer .odt.

Please also include a third person bio in your cover email.

Please note that while we'd love to feature everyone's work, releasing things via a podcast is just not possible. We only have limited time to record. We read everything, and will work with you to edit it if we want to record it.

Process

Once we've decided to record the story, we'll engage with you to perfect the piece for a recording. It's helpful if authors have read their piece out loud before we start to record it. This helps get the language right as the spoken word can be a little different from the written word.

Once you're happy with the piece, we'll start to record it. There may be changes in the script in the podcast, but we'll work with you to make sure everyone is happy.

We want to keep the rights for the audio version of the piece only. You can do whatever you want with the written piece, including publishing it elsewhere.
